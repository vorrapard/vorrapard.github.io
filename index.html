I am interested in developing robust stand-alone autonomous systems capable of solving real-world complex challenges. My expertise lies in deep learning and computer vision, which I focused on during my master's degree at NYU, conducting research under the supervision of Dr. Chinmay Hegde.

In addition to robotics, I have always been fascinated by the nature of physics and advancements in quantum computing. This passion led me to pursue a double major in physics and computer science during my undergraduate studies at Penn State, where I conducted research under the supervision of Dr. David Radice, focusing on deep learning and neutron stars.

Before entering this field, I explored various research areas, including nanomaterials and computer simulation. My work involved synthesizing silver-doped nanocarbon supercapacitor electrodes and simulating DNA behavior.
